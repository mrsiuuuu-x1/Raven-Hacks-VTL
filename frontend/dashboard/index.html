Null!fy — Glossary & Scoring Guide
Score Variables
AI Manipulation Score
The primary verdict score. Reflects how likely the image was generated or significantly altered by an AI model (e.g. Stable Diffusion, Midjourney, DALL·E, or inpainting tools). Computed by the backend neural classifier against millions of real and synthetic images.
0–29% Real
30–69% Likely AI
70–100% Definitely AI
Metadata Integrity
Measures how intact the image's EXIF metadata is. Genuine camera photos typically contain rich metadata (camera model, GPS, capture timestamp). AI-generated images and edited files often strip or lack this data entirely — raising suspicion. Higher = more trustworthy metadata.
0–39% Poor
40–69% Partial
70–100% Good
Compression Anomaly
Estimates how unusual the file's compression patterns are relative to organic camera output. AI-generated images and re-saved files often show atypical compression artefacts in their DCT coefficients and block structures. Higher score = more anomalous compression, suggesting manipulation.
0–29 Normal
30–69 Suspicious
70–100 Anomalous
EXIF Flags Explained
Camera Model
The make and model of the recording device (e.g. iPhone 15 Pro, Canon EOS R5). Missing in AI-generated images and most screenshots — flagged as suspicious when absent alongside other missing fields.
GPS Data
Geolocation coordinates embedded at capture time. Often stripped by social media platforms for privacy, but its absence alongside other missing fields raises overall suspicion.
Timestamp
The original capture date/time written by camera firmware (DateTimeOriginal). Distinct from file modification date — AI tools and editors rarely forge this field correctly.
Software
Records which application last processed or saved the file (e.g. "Adobe Photoshop 2024", "GIMP 2.10"). Its presence doesn't confirm forgery, but flags that the image passed through editing software.
SHA-256
A cryptographic fingerprint of the raw file bytes. Unique to each file version — even a single changed pixel produces a completely different hash. Used to verify integrity and detect re-saves.
Results are probabilistic. Use alongside other investigative methods.
